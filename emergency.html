Emergency Contacts

Here are some important emergency contacts and what you can do while waiting for help to arrive.

Ambulance

In case of medical emergencies requiring immediate transport, call the ambulance service.

Call Ambulance
What to do:
  • Stay calm and assess the situation.
  • If the person is injured, try to stop the bleeding by applying pressure.
  • Keep the person comfortable and still.
  • If necessary, perform CPR until the ambulance arrives.
Poison Control

If someone is poisoned, contact Poison Control immediately for assistance.

Call Poison Control
What to do:
  • Try to identify the poison and keep the packaging if possible.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless advised by a professional.
  • If the person is unconscious, do not try to give them anything to eat or drink.
  • Follow the instructions provided by Poison Control.
Fire Department

In case of fire emergencies, call the fire department for immediate assistance.

Call Fire Department
What to do:
  • If inside a building, evacuate immediately and use stairs (not the elevator).
  • If trapped inside, seal cracks with cloth to prevent smoke inhalation.
  • If clothing catches fire, stop, drop, and roll.
  • Call the fire department and provide your location and any critical details.
Police

For immediate police assistance in case of an emergency, call the police department.

Call Police
What to do:
  • Provide clear details about the situation: your location, nature of the emergency, and any suspects.
  • Do not confront any potential criminals, stay at a safe distance.
  • If safe, try to gather important details like vehicle make, model, and license plate number.
Emergency Health Hotline

For emergency health-related inquiries, you can call the health hotline.

Call Health Hotline
What to do:
  • Describe symptoms clearly if you are seeking medical advice.
  • If necessary, seek help from a healthcare provider until further instructions are given.
  • Stay calm and follow the advice of the health professional on the line.
Local Hospital

For hospital emergency services, call the nearest local hospital.

Call Local Hospital
What to do:
  • Get the person to the hospital as soon as possible if they are in critical condition.
  • If you are unable to transport them, provide the hospital with your location and request an ambulance.
  • If possible, gather relevant medical history (allergies, medications, etc.) to provide to the hospital staff.
Mental Health Support

For mental health crises, reach out to immediate support services.

Call Mental Health Support
What to do:
  • If someone is experiencing a mental health crisis, try to keep them calm and ensure their safety.
  • Listen actively without judgment, and encourage the person to seek professional help.
  • If they are in immediate danger, don't hesitate to call emergency services or a mental health professional.
